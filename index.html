Why Colmar?

Colmar is a town in northeastern France that looks like it was pulled straight from a fairy tale. With its colorful half-timbered houses, quiet canals, and surrounding vineyards, it is widely considered one of the most beautiful towns in Europe. The historic center is almost entirely preserved, and walking through its narrow streets feels like traveling back in time.

  • Location: Alsace, France — 30 minutes from Strasbourg
  • Best time to visit: May through October, or December (world-famous Christmas market)
  • How to get there: Fly to Strasbourg or Basel (Switzerland) + regional train

Tips From Those Who've Been There

Before you pack your bags, check out the advice from people who have walked every cobblestone in Colmar.

Tips:

  • Arrive early

    The historic center gets crowded after 10 AM. Wake up at sunrise to photograph the canals without the crowds.

  • Try the local Riesling

    Alsace is one of France's most famous wine regions. A glass at the end of the day is non-negotiable.

  • Wear comfortable shoes

    The streets are cobblestone. Beautiful, but unforgiving with heels.

  • Learn two words in Alsatian

    "Güete Morge" (good morning) opens many doors and earns plenty of smiles.

  • Avoid restaurants on the main square

    The best meals are found on the side streets, where the locals actually eat.
